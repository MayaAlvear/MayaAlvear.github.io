"What's in a Name?"

I ponder my own name like a long distance sister
On paper presenting ethnic but unassuming.
At age 5 I learned how to say it correctly, its phonetics foreign through the gaps of teeth in my mouth.

Soften your vowels and roll your r’s to avoid scrutiny these sounds should come naturally
It doesn’t have to be perfect but irrationally I cried because I thought that it did,
I hid behind my shame of being a no sabo kid.

When I was young I had reached for this language more times than I can count only to amount
To teasing from my grandpa that was well-meaning
but the pressure of pleasing left me with more anxiety than encouragement.

I forced myself to learn spanish through each critique,
what once was unfamiliar feels comforting now
Like how i remember my grandmother would speak.

We never spoke in her native tongue,
And i still blame myself even though I was young;
It stung that she passed without knowing who I’d become.
She was sick and i was shy
She died before I was brave enough to try
I will never make that mistake again.

I speak spanish because I’m done hiding,
And I like to think that wherever she’s residing she can hear me.
A distant call from this world reaching to her
and I’m almost sure I can feel that connection.

Sometimes still I’ll stumble through phrases i have phases where this language does not want me back.
This longing for belonging within an identity that I’ve worked to be confident to claim
All the same i find comfort in my name; like a long lost sister.

It connects me to my grandmother, connects me to my father
And when I say it I feel its power.

My name: Maya Alvear
