Queer Latin@s in STEM

A collection of individual highlights and resources for Queer Latin@s in STEM

Websites and Organizations

Notable in their field

Eduardo is a bi/pansexual Mexican American in the field of evolutionary ecology and invasive
plant biology at California State University. He is currently a masters candidate studying invasive
plant genetics, and previously attended California State University San Bernardino.
Eduardo is grateful to get to work with supportive individuals in his professional and
educational spheres, and outside of work enjoys hiking, reading, and photography.

Angie is a queer Latinx Engineer at Northrop Grumman who received a dual Bachelors of Science degree
in Electrical Engineering and Physics from New York University. She is passionate about social justice and
diversity and inclusion, and volunteers for the engineering conference Out for Undergrad (O4U) as an
admissions director. Her message to young queer people who want to pursue a career in engineering is to go
for it and to embrace the sentiment that they belong in STEM, and that others in the field will adjust to the
inclusion of identities that go against norms in the field.

Victor is a gay Brazilian Geologist and PhD student in italy at the Alma Mater Studiorum University of
Bologna. He previously studied at the Federal University of Paraná where he got his masters in Exploratory
Geology. His current work specializes in Astrobiology researching microbial carbonate precipitation in extreme
environments as a comparison to environments on Mars, although previously he has studied Geomicrobiology and
Geochemistry. Visitor first engaged with representation in academia and the sciences in his work to combat LGBTQ
oppression within his university in Brazil.

Robin Aguilar is a queer and nonbinary Latinx computational biologist and PhD candidate at University of
Washington Genome Sciences. After coming out as queer and nonbinary at a networking event at the start of
their PhD program in 2018, Robin has worked to support other scientists from marginalized communities by
writing and making art about her story and experiences, as well as establishing safe community spaces for
other trainees. Robin encourages other queer and trans scientists to realize their goals in STEM while
allowing room for self care, and in 2019 founded the Genome Sciences Association for the Inclusion of
Marginalized Students that engages in outreach at various diversity conferences and hosts events with
diverse speakers.

Agnaldo is gay and lives in Brazil where he is the head of the Graduate Program in Mathematics Education
at the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro. He has a masters in Applied and Computational Mathematics and
a PhD in Mathematics Education, and focuses on Math Education for Social Justice as he leads the research
group known as “Matematiqueer: Gender and Sexuality Studies and Mathematics Education.” Agnaldo hopes to
use his research to help women and the LGBTQ community succeed in STEM and Mathematics in Brazil. Despite
receiving pushback for seemingly politicizing a field regarded as neutral and apolitical, he calls for gender
and LGBTQ equity in STEM and partners with non-governmental organizations on social justice projects through
MatematiQueer.

Celine Cuevas is a queer Mexican, Taiwanese, and Philipino American and a product manager at Redfin. She
studied Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Yale University, and since her sophomore year has
attended and been involved with O4U (Out 4 Undergrad) conferences. At Redfin and in her professional life
she advocates for more LGBTQ and racial diversity and representation in tech, and started an employee
resource group called People of Color in Tech (PoCiT) in her office in San Francisco.

Raul is a gay Mexican American chemistry professor at Occidental college where he serves as a mentor and
representation for younger underrepresented people hoping to go into STEM. He notes that in his career he
has faced difficulties as a result of his identity as both queer and Mexican American, but is happy to teach
chemistry and lead research while addressing systemic barriers to STEM that people in his community are
faced with.
